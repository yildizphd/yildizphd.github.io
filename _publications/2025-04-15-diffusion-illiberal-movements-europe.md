excerpt: 'How do illiberal protest movements diffuse, and what factors influence their spread? Drawing on diffusion literature, I identify learning and emulation as key mechanisms, whereby actors exchange information and adopt strategies from other movements. I argue that perceived grievances and legitimacy link these mechanisms: learning provides ideas and tactics, but the validation of grievances and illiberal beliefs enables confident emulation. I identify immigration flows and economic hardship as sources of grievances, which create triggering conditions and make countries more susceptible to diffusion. Using original data from 41 European countries (1995–2020), I show that illiberal protest activity in one country becomes more likely when similar mobilizations occur elsewhere, regardless of geographic proximity. Economic hardship amplifies this effect, whereas refugee inflows, contrary to expectations, have no noticeable impact.'
